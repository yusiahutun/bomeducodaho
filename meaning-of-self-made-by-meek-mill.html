The Meek Mill song titled 'Self Made' is a powerful anthem that delves into the idea of personal growth, determination, and resilience. In this article, we will explore the various themes presented in this song and analyze some standout lyrics that contribute to the overall message.At its core, 'Self Made' embraces the concept of being in control of one's destiny and pursuing success despite the obstacles stacked against us. The title itself speaks volumes about the song's message: it's not about relying on others or waiting for opportunities to come knocking, but rather about taking charge and creating those opportunities for oneself.One of the standout lyrics in the song that resonates deeply with the overarching theme is "I made it myself, guess I'm self-made." This lyric emphasizes the artist's journey of rising above adverse circumstances, starting from the bottom, and making it to the top through sheer determination and hard work. It reflects the idea that true success is earned, not handed out, and reinforces the importance of self-belief.Another compelling lyric that adds depth to the song's theme is "Motivated just from being broke." This line sheds light on the idea that sometimes it is the hardships we face that give us the drive to excel. It suggests that being pushed to our limits can serve as a powerful motivation to break free from our circumstances and achieve greatness. This lyric highlights the transformative power of embracing struggle.As the song progresses, Meek Mill's lyrics become more introspective and introspective. He reflects on the challenges he has faced and how they have shaped him, stating, "When people used to ask if I was high, I would tell them no, 'Cause I was never hesitant to represent." This lyric conveys the artist's resilience and his refusal to let negativity or adversity define him. It underscores the importance of staying true to oneself and remaining focused on the path to success, no matter what others may say or think.Additionally, the lyric "They lock me in the cell just for selling dope" speaks to the experiences of discrimination and injustice faced by many individuals who come from disadvantaged backgrounds. This line delves into the broader issue of systemic inequalities that can hinder personal growth and highlight the need for systemic change. It adds a layer of social consciousness to the song's message, urging listeners to reflect on the unequal opportunities available to different communities.As the song continues, Pill enters the scene with a verse that reinforces the theme of self-determination. His lyric, "We self-made, carry on while we carry out," embodies the idea that even in the face of adversity, one must remain steadfast and continue to pursue their goals. It showcases the resilience and drive required to overcome obstacles and succeed on one's terms.In conclusion, 'Self Made' by Meek Mill is an empowering anthem that celebrates taking control of one's destiny and forging a path to success through hard work and determination. The various standout lyrics discussed in this article collectively reinforce the overarching theme and present unexpected and imaginative themes. From embracing struggle as a motivator to rising above systemic inequalities, this song encapsulates the essence of personal growth and resilience. It serves as a reminder to embrace one's power and work toward becoming self-made.
