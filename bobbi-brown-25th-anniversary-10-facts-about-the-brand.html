10 facts about Bobbi Brown to mark 25 years

June 2024 · 3 minute read

courtesy of Bobbi Brown

She started one of the biggest beauty brands to date, she's penned eight books and she's a staunch advocate of clean living, there's almost nothing Bobbi Brown can't turn into gold. This year, the make-up artist and the brand, which bears her name, will celebrate 25 years of success within an ever changing industry. To mark this special landmark anniversary, Bazaar put together ten interesting facts about Bobbi Brown…   

The breakthrough moment: "It was as a make-up artist on a Vogue cover with Naomi Campbell, shot by Patrick Demarchelier. I was so excited to get that job. It was my first cover shoot and such a legendary one for Vogue with Naomi on the cover. I was so nervous and did make-up on Naomi that was out of her comfort zone, but in the end everything went really well. I could never have predicted at that time that I'd eventually have my own line. I was just living in the moment."

The history: With just five thousand dollars and a simple line of 10 lipsticks, Bobbi Brown cosmetics launched 25 years ago in Bergdorf Goodman in New York. There are now about 1,199 SKUs and 242 core products total.

The approach: The healthy, natural-looking ethos to make-up that the brand has become renowned for, initially started as a backlash against the 'more is more' look of the Eighties, which Bobbi Brown disapproved of.

The takeover: When Estee Lauder Companies acquired Bobbi Brown in 1995, Leonard Lauder told Bobbi "I want you to keep doing what you're doing" – and she has ever since.

The bestseller: The Long-Wear Gel Eyeliner, £19, sells more units globally than any other Bobbi Brown product with two sold every minute.

The Mike Tyson moment: She convinced Mike Tyson to wear make-up… "He said, "you ain't touching me with that stuff." I said, "OK," and then I said, "Do you want to look shiny"? And he said, "no," I said, "OK, then let me touch up.""

The shows: While Bobbi Brown has never followed the seasonal beauty trends, she is a regular backstage at the shows. "Throughout the years, I've worked with numerous designers, including Roland Mouret, L'Wren Scott, Tory Burch, Stella McCartney, etc.  During the last two seasons, I have loved working Tibi, Marchesa and Jenny Packham," says Bobbi.

The philanthropic philosophy: Three years ago, Bobbi Brown launched The Pretty Powerful campaign on International Women's Day and donates profits to organisations that empower women through job skills training and education programmes. The brand is also heavily involved with the Breast Cancer Awareness month every October.

courtesy of Bobbi Brown

The icon: The Shimmer Brick, £34, is one of the most iconic beauty products, but it's story isn't what you'd necessarily assume. "One year at fashion week a designer asked me to give all the models a 'lit-from-within' glow. I took a shimmery eyeshadow and dusted it on the models' cheeks and that's how the concept for Shimmer Brick was born," explains Bobbi.

The numbers: Today, the brand sells more than 23 million products a year, the equivalent of about 2,735 products an hour, or 46 every minute. Bobbi Brown cosmetics are available in 74 countries and sold in more than 2173 stores.
