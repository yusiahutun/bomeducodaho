A Hidden Paradise in the Caribbean

Los Roques Archipelago is a hidden gem located in the Caribbean Sea, off the coast of Venezuela. Comprised of more than 350 islands and islets, this untouched paradise offers pristine beaches, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and vibrant coral reefs teeming with marine life.

The Largest Marine Park in the Caribbean

Did you know that Los Roques Archipelago is home to the largest marine park in the Caribbean? The National Park of Los Roques covers approximately 40% of the total area of the archipelago, making it a haven for biodiversity and a protected sanctuary for numerous species of birds, turtles, and fish.

Ideal Destination for Water Sports Enthusiasts

With its calm and warm waters, Los Roques Archipelago is a paradise for water sports enthusiasts. From snorkeling and scuba diving to windsurfing and kitesurfing, visitors can immerse themselves in a variety of exhilarating aquatic activities while exploring the stunning underwater world.

A Paradise for Birdwatchers

Los Roques Archipelago is a haven for birdwatchers, with over 90 species of birds inhabiting the islands. From colorful flamingos to magnificent frigatebirds, bird enthusiasts can witness a diverse array of avian species in their natural habitat.

Exclusive Luxury Resorts

Preserving the Ecosystem

The Venezuelan government has taken significant steps to preserve the pristine environment of Los Roques Archipelago. FAQs

1. How do I get to Los Roques Archipelago?

There are regular flights from Caracas, Venezuela’s capital, to the main island of Gran Roque. The flight takes approximately 35 minutes and operates several times a day.

2. Are there accommodations available on the islands?

Yes, there are a variety of accommodations available on Gran Roque, ranging from luxury resorts to guesthouses and boutique hotels. However, it’s recommended to book in advance, especially during the high season.

3. Can I go scuba diving in Los Roques Archipelago?

Absolutely! Los Roques Archipelago is a popular destination for scuba diving. There are several dive centers on Gran Roque that offer guided dives for divers of all levels of experience.

4. Is it possible to visit the other islands of the archipelago?

Yes, you can explore the other islands of Los Roques Archipelago by taking day trips or hiring a boat. Each island has its own unique charm and beauty, so it’s worth exploring as much as you can during your visit.

5. What is the best time to visit Los Roques Archipelago?

The best time to visit Los Roques Archipelago is during the dry season, which lasts from December to April. During this time, you can enjoy sunny days, calm waters, and excellent visibility for snorkeling and diving.

6. Are there any restrictions in place to protect the environment?

Yes, as a protected national park, there are certain rules and regulations in place to preserve the archipelago’s pristine environment. Visitors are required to pay an entrance fee and follow guidelines regarding waste disposal and respect for the local ecosystems.

7. Is Los Roques Archipelago suitable for families?

Absolutely! Los Roques Archipelago offers a safe and family-friendly environment. The calm waters, shallow beaches, and abundance of marine life make it an ideal destination for families with children of all ages.

8. Are there any water sports activities available?

Yes, Los Roques Archipelago offers a wide range of water sports activities such as snorkeling, kayaking, kiteboarding, and paddleboarding. There is something for everyone, whether you’re a beginner or an experienced water sports enthusiast.

9. Can I bring my own food and drinks to the islands?

While there are restaurants and small stores on Gran Roque where you can purchase food and drinks, you are also allowed to bring your own provisions to the islands. However, it’s important to be mindful of waste management and dispose of any trash responsibly.
