Alexander started racing go-carts when he was ten and became interested in the sport. His father founded an investment fund to raise the money needed for his career. He then founded Team Rossi Motorsports and One Motorsports Marketing and Management to advance his son’s career.

Alexander attended Forest Lake Christian School and graduated when he was sixteen. He then joined Sierra College, majoring in Business Administration and Management. He obtained his Associate of Arts degree in 2011.

Career beginnings

Alexander Rossi's career can be traced back to 2005 when he became the IKF Grand National Champion in the 100cc Yamaha class. He was also the semi-finalist in the Red Bull Formula One American Drivers search, finishing in the top five of the more than 2,000 candidates.

By 2006, he had already been awarded the Skip Barber National Scholarship from Skip Barber Racing School. This gave him entry into the 2006 Skip Barber National Championship, where he finished third overall and became the youngest championship winner at fourteen.

Post formula BMW career

In 2007, Rossi competed in the Formula BMW USA series and finished in third position. He returned in 2008 and won the overall championship. He finished the season as world champion, winning the 2008 Formula BMW World Final at the Autodromo Hermanos Rodriguez circuit in Mexico City.

After his triumph, Rossi decided to move to Europe in 2009 to compete there. He chose to compete in the International Formula Master representing Hitech Racing. Soon after, he moved to ISR Racing and won three races during the season. The GP3 Series welcomed Rossi into its competition in 2010 as he joined the multiple-championship-winning team ART Grand Prix.

His next destination was the Formula Renault 3.5 Series with Fortec Motorsport, where he teamed up with Cesar Ramos. He finished third in the championship and as the top rookie driver. He competed in the series in 2012 but represented Arden Caterham Motorsport.

Formula one and sports car racing career

After a stint in the GP2 Series, Rossi was contracted to be the reserve and test driver for US F1, but the move did not go through. In 2012, he became a test driver for the Caterham F1 team. He participated in the first practice session at the United States Grand Prix and once again drove the Caterham in the first practice session of the Canadian Grand Prix in 2014. He left Caterham in July 2014.

Rossi made his debut in Sports car racing at the 24 Hours of Le Mans, having been signed as a replacement for Christian Zugel. In 2019 and 2020, Rossi joined Team Penske for both 24 Hours of Daytona and 12 Hours of Sebring in the INSA WeatherTech SportsCar Championship.
