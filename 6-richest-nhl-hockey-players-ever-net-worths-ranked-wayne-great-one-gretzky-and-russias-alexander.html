6. Chris Pronger

5. Pavel Bure

Estimated net worth: US$70 million

“Russian Rocket” Pavel Bure is highly regarded for his speed, skill and puck possession. The Vancouver Canucks legend is known for being private about his personal life and even after retirement is one of the most inaccessible players in the game.

Per The Athletic, Bure said he loved to entertain the crowd: “What I loved most was scoring goals. And not just goals, but beautiful goals that fans would remember.”

4. Sidney Crosby

Estimated net worth: US$75 million

The Canadian player is known to turn down endorsement deals, only working with a select few partners including Gatorade, CCM and Tim Hortons. Nicknamed “Sid the Kid”, his leadership and on-ice achievements have made him one of the sport’s most popular players.

The athlete and his partner of 15 years, Kathy Leutner, keep a low profile and off social media – meaning that fans go gaga on the rare occasion the good-looking pair are photographed.

3. Alexander Ovechkin

Estimated net worth: US$80 million

After being drafted by the Washington Capitals in 2004, Ovechkin stayed with the team for his entire career. His dominance and high scoring rate have led to him being considered one of the sport’s greatest players. Ovechkin has made big money off the field too, with endorsement deals from Nike, Bauer and PepsiCo.

2. Mario Lemieux

Estimated net worth: US$200 million

This Canadian hockey icon raked up impressive stats during his 17-season career. Known as “Le Magnifique” and “Super Mario”, Lemieux’s most lucrative deals included a 1992 seven-year guaranteed deal worth US$42 million with the Pittsburgh Penguins.

Lemieux was so invested in his team that, in 1999, he agreed to convert US$20 million of his owed salary to ownership equity to prevent the Penguins from going into bankruptcy.

Also prompting deep respect from fans and colleagues was the fact that Lemieux was diagnosed with Hodgkin’s lymphoma and, in 1993, returned to active play after 22 radiation treatments.

1. Wayne Gretzky

Estimated net worth: US$250 million

Known as “the Great One”, Canada’s Wayne Gretzky has been called the GOAT (Greatest of All Time) by sportswriters, players and the NHL itself. After playing professionally for over two decades, Gretzky retired in 1999 with over 60 NHL records under his belt. His incredible ability has made him the richest player on our list, with investments in property, a vineyard and sports team ownership.
