1920s actress Louise Brooks is credited with popularizing the bob hairstyle during the flapper era. But her sharp chin-grazing cut wouldn't have been complete without the blunt bangs that fell right above her eyebrows.

Clara Bow

Clara Bow's short and messy bob was reminiscent of bedhead and full of volume. The 1920s actress topped her look off with light piecey bangs across her forehead.

3

Bette Davis

John Kobal Foundation//Getty Images

When film star Bette Davis got her start on-screen in the early 1930s, she was known for her signature curled mini bangs. She changed up her style throughout her career, but went back to bangs in the 1960s.

4

Audrey Hepburn

Corbis

Audrey Hepburn has often served as an inspiration for style and elegance, but throughout the years one of her hairstyles stands out among the rest. In 1950, the actress debuted a pixie haircut with baby bangs that quickly became the decade's most popular cut.

5

Bettie Page

Michael Ochs Archives//Getty Images

Bettie Page has long been remembered for more than just her pin-up days in the 1950s. In fact, the model's short arched bangs were so unique they were coined Bettie Bangs and are still in demand decades later.

6

Natalie Wood

Silver Screen Collection//Getty Images

In the 1950s, child actress Natalie Wood wanted to transition into more adult roles, but the studio and her fans found it difficult to separate her look from the little girl they watched in Miracle on 34th Street. Wood changed that by debuting a short bob with micro bangs in 1955 and landing a role in Rebel Without a Cause that same year.

7

Mia Farrow

George Rinhart//Getty Images

In 1960, Mia Farrow took a pair of scissors to her long blonde hair—the hairstyle that made her the classic girl-next-door type on her TV show Peyton Place. The result was an iconic pixie cut with equally as daring short bangs.

Diana Ross

Michael Ochs Archives//Getty Images

Before Diana Ross's successful solo career, the singer rose to fame as a member The Supremes with chic arched bangs that fell just below her eyebrows. The Motown group became one of the most successful of the decade and Ross's look helped define the era's modern style.

Jane Birkin

Jane Birkin's shaggy bangs in the 1960s were a huge part of the model's effortlessly chic charm and they continue to serve as bang inspiration to this day.

Jacqueline Kennedy

If there was one thing you could count on in 1961, it was a pillbox cap perfectly positioned on Jackie Kennedy's coiffed hair. The First Lady of the United States has long been revered for her fashion sense, which includes her immaculately blown-out bangs.

Anna Karina
