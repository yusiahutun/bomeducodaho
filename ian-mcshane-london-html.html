Ian McShane, 80, shows off his new whitened teeth and slimline frame as he steps out in London

April 2024 · 4 minute read

He is better known for sporting a mane of shaggy long locks.

And while Ian McShane - who played the brutal brothel keeper in TV western Deadwood - has rocked that look for years, he has now decided to change it up.

The actor, 80, stepped out in London on Friday showcasing a much shorter hairstyle, brand new whitened teethm and a slimline new frame.

The Game of Thrones star wore a black shirt with patterned detailing, which he teamed with a black jacket and jeans and finished off his ensemble a pair of sunglasses.

He donned a pair of stylish black trainers for the outing and beamed from ear to ear as he was spotted laughing and joking with a friend, embracing them in a hug. 

Different look: Ian McShane, 80, was worlds away from his famous shaggy mane (right) as he showed off his new clean-cut look and whitened teeth during an outing in London on Friday

Spotted: The actor displayed a shorter head of hair and slender figure as he stepped out to meet a friend in the capital

Ian has carved out a hugely successful 52-year career in TV film and theatre on both sides of the Atlantic.

He graced the screen in the 90s as the antiques dealer in the hit BBC show Lovejoy where he appeared in 71 episodes.

His role was the protagonist in the comedy drama, which ran for eight years and had six successful series.

Fans of the show named him the 'irresistible rogue' where he sported impressive dark locks and a clean-shaven face – notably different from his current look.

The star who won a Golden Globe for his role in Deadwood also played the deliciously wicked Blackbeard in a Pirates Of The Caribbean movie.

He has also appeared on screen as a corrupt bishop in the 2010 series Pillars Of The Earth, appeared in Miami Vice, American Horror Story: Asylum and The Simpsons.

Most recently he appeared in John Wick Chapter 4 where he sported a silver beard.

In the film Ian plays John Wick's friend and the manager of the Continental which is a hotel for criminals.

In 1980, Ian chose to go sober and gave up alcohol after years of partying, but during his wild ways, the Deadwood actor developed a reputation as a ladies' man.

His first marriage to Suzan Farmer ended after two years, while his second to Ruth Post – who is the mother his children Kate and Morgan – ended after it emerged he had been having an affair.

He is now married to Gwen Humble, who he describes as 'the love of my life'.

The couple tied the knot in 1980.
