New York's Best Spots for Mulled Wine

June 2024 · 3 minute read

With temperatures dropping, warm drinks are becoming just as much of a chic necessity as turtlenecks and tailored jackets. While hot chocolate and steaming coffee are always great ideas during cold times, we're looking to red mulled wine for our cocktail of the season. It's versatile, zesty, sweet and the color—a deep, lush burgundy—is undeniably gorgeous in contrast to the changing fall leaves. Here, six of the best spots to try the beverage in New York City.

1) Middle Branch: The hidden Murray Hill bar is located in a former antiques store space and has sweeping views of the gorgeously glow-y Empire State Building. Go for the elegant atmosphere—and try their version of red mulled wine, with fresh lemon juice, allspice liqueur and Domaine de Canton Ginger Liqueur, all garnished with a cinnamon stick and brandied cherries

2) The Odeon: The retro neon lights of Odeon scream of 80s New York in the best possible way. The Odeon, which is considered a downtown New York City institution for many, is perfect for people watching, with its creative crowd, and the mulled wine—tangy, and garnished with an orange slice—is great for sipping at a corner table on a crisp fall day.

3) Black Mountain Wine House: It doesn't get much cozier than red mulled wine served warm, while seated near a quaint fireplace. Black Mountain Wine House in Gowanus, Brooklyn serves a simple but delicious concoction with house made clove syrup. The exterior of the restaurant looks like a chic and toasty cabin.

Media Platforms Design Team

4) Buvette: In terms of Euro-style brunching, Buvette has made a name for itself with its stylish interior and delicious French fare. The eatery also has a sister restaurant in Paris. The West Village based bistro serves a sweet and zesty mulled red wine, which can be enjoyed on chilly late nights. Buvette stays open until 2 A.M!

Media Platforms Design Team

5) Jadis: The Lower East Side's coziest, chicest wine bar also offers red mulled wine in a casual, fun space. Try a spicy, flavorful glass with small plates of tasty French bites like mushroom truffle risotto puffs, charcuterie, quiche Lorraine or Alsacienne tartes flambees, all fit for the cooler weather.

6) Café Gitane: Located both in the West Village and in NoLita, Café Gitane may be most well-known for their Instagram-famous avocado toast and latte art. But the restaurant, which focuses on French-Moroccan food, also serves a delish red mulled wine made with Chateau Recougne 2010 Bordeaux with its rich cherry and plum fruit flavor along with a hint of cinnamon and cloves.
