Lux Pascal Embracing Identity as Transgender Activist

September 2024 · 4 minute read

Lux Pascal, originally Lucas Balmaceda, is an American-Chilean actress and transgender advocate. She openly identified as a transgender woman in February 2021, passionately supporting the transgender community.

Lux Pascal (@luxpascal_), born on June 4, 1992, in California, is the younger sibling of actor Pedro Pascal. Lux publicly came out as a transgender woman in February 2021, sharing her journey in a cover story for Ya magazine. She began hormone treatment in July of the previous year, expressing gratitude for the strong support from her family, particularly her brother Pedro, who openly championed her transition.

Before embracing her womanhood, Lux identified as non-binary and initially came out as a gay man. A graduate of The Juilliard School’s MFA Acting program in May 2023, Lux has made a name for herself in acting with notable roles in Narcos, La Jauría, and La California. She remains a dedicated advocate for the transgender and non-binary communities.

Lux Pascal is a positive force, both in her personal journey and in her commitment to advocating for transgender and non-binary communities.

Lux Pascal A Trailblazing Actress and Transgender Advocate

Lux Balmaceda Pascal, an American with Chilean roots, is a renowned actress and transgender activist. Known for her contributions to the entertainment industry, Lux gained public attention when she came out as a transgender woman in February 2021, sharing her experiences and actively contributing to transgender activism.

In addition to her successful acting career, Lux is a powerful voice for the transgender cause. Beyond her artistic endeavors, she uses her platform to champion understanding and acceptance. Lux Pascal stands out as a significant figure, leaving her mark in both the entertainment world and the advocacy for transgender rights and visibility.

Lux Pascal’s Journey From Exile to Transgender Advocacy at 31

As of 2024, Lux Pascal, born Lucas Balmaceda on June 4, 1992, in California, is 31 years old. The youngest among four siblings, including actor Pedro Pascal, Lux’s family faced exile from Chile after the 1973 coup due to her mother’s political connections. Lux’s childhood in California shifted to Chile at age three, attending Saint George’s College.

Starting her theater studies in 2010 at the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile, Lux later earned her MFA in acting from Juilliard School in May 2023. Lux publicly embraced her identity as a transgender woman in February 2021, adopting her maternal surname. In a relationship with actor José Antonio Raffo since 2011, Lux Pascal’s journey encompasses her upbringing, education, and personal milestones that have shaped her into the actress and transgender advocate she is today.

Lux Pascal’s Ascension From Theater Debut to International Stardom

In 2014, during Lux Pascal’s fourth year of theater studies, she made her stage debut in Pablo Rotemberg’s play “La noche obstinada.” Her television journey commenced the same year with the role of “Axel Miller” in Canal 13’s series “Los 80.” Lux continued her TV success, co-starring in TVN’s “Juana Brava” in 2015 and appearing in Canal 13’s “Veinteañero a los 40” the next year.

Entering the film scene in 2016 with “Prueba de actitud,” directed by Fabrizio Copano and Augusto Matte, Lux expanded her horizons by playing Elijah in the third season of Netflix’s “Narcos” in 2017, alongside her brother Pedro. Lux’s career flourished in 2019 with roles in three Chilean film productions: “No quiero ser tu hermano,” “Ella es Cristina,” and “The Prince.”
