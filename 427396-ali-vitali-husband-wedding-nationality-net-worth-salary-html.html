Professional career

Ali Vitali was first involved in the professional world when she was still at Tulane University. She interned at Vera Wang for four months between May and August 2009. In 2010, she worked for three months as an Ad Sales and Marketing Intern, Primetime and Late Night TV for NBC Universal, Inc.

Between May and August 2011, Vitali worked as a Research Intern, Late Night with Jimmy Fallon for NBC Universal, Inc. Between May and September 2012, Ali Vitali worked for ABC News as a Production and Development Assistant. After leaving ABC News in September, she started working for Sweet Lemon Media as the Vice President and Managing Editor.

Ali managed contributors and editors, wrote weekly columns for Sweet Lemon’s website, bi-monthly columns for Sweet Lemon’s digital magazine, and hosted and produced segments for Sweet Lemon TV.

However, she left the position in 2014 after working for the media house for two years. She started working for MSNBC in 2013 after working as an Associate Editor Intern between September and December 2012.
