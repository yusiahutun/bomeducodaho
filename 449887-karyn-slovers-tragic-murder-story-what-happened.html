Who is Karyn Slover?

Karyn is the woman who was murdered on September 27th, 1996, in Decatur, Illinois. Her body parts were found in plastic bags in and around Lake Shelbyville. Investigation into the murder implicated her ex-husband and in-laws.

Hearn was born on October 27th, 1972, in Champaign County, Illinois. She was the daughter of Larry and Donna Hearn. She was only 23 years old when she made her cruel and untimely demise.

Karyn was married to Michael Slover Jr., and the two had a son called Kolten. However, since he was sometimes violent with her, she ended the marriage. She was dating David Swann before she was murdered.

What happened to Karyn Slover from Decatur IL?

On September 27th, 1996, Karyn left work at Decatur Herald & Review newspaper. She had plans to pick up her son and then shop for a dress at the Hickory Point Mall. Unfortunately, that was the last time her workmates saw her alive.

On the night of the eventful day, a police officer found a black Pontiac Bonneville in central Illinois on westbound I-72. Karyn Slover's murder investigation

The post-mortem report revealed that she had been shot seven times in the head. It also revealed that her body was dismembered using a power saw. Her boyfriend, David Swann, was the first suspect because he had some legal issues in the past. He cooperated with investigators and was cleared of the charges.

In 1998 investigators implicated Karyn's ex-father-in-law Michael Slover Sr. The broken concrete at Miracle Motors, Michael's business, matched those used to weigh down Hearn's body. They also discovered that Karyn and her ex-husband's family didn't get along.

Hearn had won custody of Kolten in the divorce. However, the court ruled that her in-laws would have the right to babysit him while Karyn was at work. Karyn did not like it, which was the reason for their restrained relationship. Jeanette Slover, Michael Slover Jr.'s mother, reportedly hated her.

The Slovers were arrested and charged with first-degree murder in January 2000. A Macon County jury found all three guilty, and a judge sentenced them each to at least 60 years in prison. They appealed their murder sentences but lost the appeal in June 2003.
