FAQs

Q: Is marshmallow creme the same as marshmallows?

A: No, marshmallow creme and marshmallows are different. Marshmallow creme is a spreadable, fluffy mixture commonly used in recipes. Marshmallows, on the other hand, are individually shaped and often enjoyed on their own or roasted over a fire.

Q: Is marshmallow creme gluten-free?

A: Marshmallow creme is typically gluten-free. However, it is always advisable to check the label carefully to ensure it doesn’t contain any gluten-containing ingredients or potential cross-contamination.

Q: How should I store marshmallow creme?

A: Marshmallow creme should be stored in a cool and dry place, away from direct sunlight. It is best to keep it tightly sealed in its original container or transferred to an airtight container to maintain freshness.

Q: Can marshmallow creme be used as a substitute for marshmallows in recipes?

A: Yes, marshmallow creme can be used as a substitute for marshmallows in certain recipes. However, keep in mind that the texture and consistency may vary. Adjustments may be needed when using marshmallow creme as a replacement.

Q: Can marshmallow creme be frozen?

A: Yes, marshmallow creme can be frozen. Freezing may change the texture slightly, but it remains safe to eat. To thaw, simply place it in the refrigerator overnight or let it sit at room temperature until it becomes spreadable again.
