Alicia Vikander on beauty, confidence &amp; love

January 2024 · 2 minute read

"Love is the most important thing of all," Alicia Vikander muses, poetically. We're on the set of her Harper's Bazaar cover shoot in Paris, and she's sharing what she's discovered about beauty, confidence, feminism and more in the latest episode of our Life Lessons franchise.

She continues: "I think maybe that becomes more apparent for each day that I grow older. The things that I realise I care about more and more are relationships and the people I have around me." She pauses, and laughs self-deprecatingly. "If you can follow the path of love, I think you're on a good one."

Betina Du Toit

The Oscar-winning actress says that in entering her thirties (she's now 33) and becoming a mother, she's also learnt how to worry less and, as a result, feels more self-assured.

"Having doubt or self-doubt is something that is just human and is always going to be there," she says. "I'm much more kind to myself now. If I would have met me like 10, 15 years ago, I would have just said, 'It's OK. It's going to be fine.' I've worried about everything – my choices, if I said the right thing, if I did the right thing – and that itself takes up a lot of time and energy."

"Then comes a time when that confidence has settled within you, hopefully, and feels kind of natural."

But one thing she's always known, from a very young age, she says, is that she wanted to perform. Her latest project is a Sky Atlantic television series called Irma Vep, written and directed by the French filmmaker Olivier Assayas. In it, she plays Mira, an American film star who comes to Paris to act in a remake of Les Vampires, a French silent classic about a criminal gang, whose antiheroine is the wholly amoral, black-catsuited femme fatale Irma Vep (an anagram of vampire).

Betina Du Toit

"I grew up with a mother who's an actress, so I got introduced to that world very early," Vikander explains. She recalls how she took part in a miming contest for children back in Sweden when she was about seven years old – a video recording of which she watched again recently. "In that, I'm asked, 'So what do you want to do when you grow up?' And I just say, 'I'm going to become an actress.'"
