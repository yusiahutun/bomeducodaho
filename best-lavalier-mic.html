Lavalier microphones, also called lapel microphones, are small, lightweight clip-on mics for capturing hands-free audio. They clip directly onto your clothing, remaining a constant distance from the subject's mouth for consistent results.

But you don't need to spend a considerable amount of money on a lavalier microphone. Instead, you can pick up a cheap lavalier mic, and most people won't realize you're using a low-cost mic.

Here are the best budget lavalier microphones available today.

FAQ

Q: What Should I Look for When Buying a Lavalier Microphone?

Lavalier microphones are clipped and positioned the same way, but the connectors are adaptable for different devices. You'll need a lavalier mic with the same connector as your device's mic input jack.

If you're using a smartphone, tablet, or laptop, you'll need a lavalier microphone with a 3.5mm (1/8-inch) TRRS connector. It looks similar to the connector found on cameras, but they are different.

Most DSLR and digital cameras and recorders use a 3.5mm (1/8-inch) TRS mic input jack. A TRRS connector has three black rings, while TRS has two.

For computers, an iPhone, or the latest smartphones, look for a lavalier mic with a USB, Lightning, or USB-C connector. In some cases, you use an adapter to connect a lavalier mic with different devices.

Q: Are Cheap Lavalier Mics Any Good?

Lavalier mics used to come at a premium, but lately, we've seen cheap and inexpensive lavalier microphones with excellent sound quality and clarity. Established brands such as Shure, Rode, and Sony offer superb lavalier mics that don't break the bank.

Q: Does a Lavalier Mic Need Power?

Like all microphones, a lavalier mic needs power to operate. Some lavalier mics come with built-in or replaceable batteries, but most rely on plug-in power, meaning the host device or recorder provides the required power to the mic. In this case, the mic itself won't require batteries.

If you're buying an XLR lavalier mic for a USB audio interface or mixers, make sure your device can supply 48V phantom power.
