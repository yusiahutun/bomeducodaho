Eerie moment masked Oregon man smashes Rice Northwest Museum of Rocks and Minerals window and flees

June 2024 Â· 2 minute read

A suspect smashed through the window of an Oregon museum and stole several gold pieces early on Sunday morning, as police are stumped at finding the culprit. 

Footage of the alarming incident shows the man approaching the small Rice Northwest Museum of Rocks and Minerals around 2.30am Sunday and smashing a window before hopping right into the building.

Deputies with the Washington County Sheriff's Office followed footprints heading south from the smashed window and museum. But could not find the suspect. 

Deputies attempted to use a trained police dog to track the suspect, but were not successful.

Around 2.30am on Sunday morning, a man clad in all black, including a ski mask, used a sledgehammer to smash his way into a small museum on Washington County, Oregon and stole 'numerous' pieces of gold

Inside the museum, officials found a dramatically smashed display case from which 'numerous gold items' had been taken.

Deputies are not currently releasing the value of the missing items.

The suspect is described as an average-built white man who was wearing jeans, a jacket and a mask.

Glass cases that held pieces of natural gold, among other things, were violently smashed with the suspect's sledgehammer and their contents removed.

Some of the gold pieces appeared to be unaltered rock-like specimens found in Oregon, California, and other parts of the American West.

Other pieces were anchored in other historical contexts, including thousands of years old rings and pieces of jewelry from the Middle East.

The pieces of value seemed to be labeled quite clearly - perhaps something the museum directors are now rethinking. 

The word 'GOLD!!' is printed in large font in the glass case that was smashed and robbed. 

The display case prominently labeled 'GOLD!!' was smashed and stolen from during the Sunday robbery

Each room in the museum celebrates a different type of rock

The small museum was founded in 1952 and operates to this day out of the home of its founders, Richard and Helen Rice.

Nearly 30 years ago, the museum was formally reestablished as a non-profit.

Its collection included more than 20,000 rock and mineral specimens. Each room celebrates a different kind of rock.

Anyone with information about the potential suspect or the burglary is asked to get in touch with the Washington County Sheriff's Office at 503-846-2700.
