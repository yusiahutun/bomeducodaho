Muliro Gardens: Kakamega Park Sheds Infamous Reputation, Revamped by County Government

March 2024 · 3 minute read
  • In 2011, Muliro Gardens, a public park at the centre of Kakamega town, became a household name in Kenya for all the wrong reasons
  • Photos of randy couples who had turned the bamboo benches into love beds surfaced online, causing a saga
  • 11 years after the incident, TUKO.co.ke visited the park to see the changes made as a result of the saga

An expose by the press with images depicting couples in compromising positions at Kakamega’s Muliro Gardens grabbed headlines in 2011.

Muliro Gardens’ infamity

Kakamega residents were equally shocked by the daring daytime escapades as the park is smack in the middle of town, sandwiched between two busy roads and right next to the county government offices.

Such a negative picture for a park in honour of one of Luhya Land’s greatest leaders, Masinde Muliro, sprung the government and church into action.

Preachers from the area first flocked to the park with holy water to cleanse the benches with prayers.

Under Governor Wycliffe Oparanya, the county government built a fence around the area with two gated entrances- one facing the Kakamega- Kisumu highway and the other opposite the Co-Operative bank.

In addition, the thick bamboo groves which provided cover for the uncouth acts were trimmed.

Visiting Muliro Gardens 11 years later

Fast-forward to 22, the infamous incident is almost forgotten, and the park is almost unrecognisable.

Carbro footpaths erected by the county government make walking around easier, and business people are making a killing.

Evans Karamoja, a young man who works as a professional photographer in the park, admitted business is good. TUKO.co.ke found him busy at work armed with a camera and professional lights.

“Most of our clients are university students,” he disclosed.

Karamoja admitted that some of his clients still inquire about the infamous benches.

Hildah Mukwana also plies her trade selling local cuisines, including makhalanje and the mukhombero root, long rumoured to increase virility in men.

The mother of two said the 2011 incident affected business as restrictions were imposed on the number of businesses operating in the park.

“Thankfully, Oparanya allowed us back in.”

Amazing sights of Kakamega

Elsewhere, TUKO.co.ke highlighted some of the most scenic areas one can visit in the county.

The Kakamega Forest, for instance, has stood the test of time as a portion of the rainforest that stretched across Central Africa.

The legendary crying stone of Ilesi, which has a person's likeliness, is another famous landmark.
