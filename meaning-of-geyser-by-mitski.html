Meaning of Geyser by Mitski

June 2024 Â· 3 minute read

In Mitski's mesmerizing song "Geyser," we are taken on a journey through the depths of emotion, exploring themes of desire, sacrifice, and the complexities of love. The song's overarching theme revolves around the intensity of an all-consuming love and the inherent sacrifices one is willing to make for it. Let's delve deeper into the standout lyrics that Mitski masterfully weaves into the fabric of this emotional ballad.The opening verse immediately sets the stage for the song's core theme, proclaiming, "You're my number one, you're the one I want, and I've turned down every hand that has beckoned me to come." This lyric reflects a profound commitment to a specific person, implying that the protagonist has chosen this individual above all others. It highlights the desire and longing felt by someone who has found their soulmate and the sacrifices they have made for the sake of this unique connection.The pre-chorus lyric, "'Cause you're the one I got, you're the one I got, so I'll keep turning down the hands that beckon me to come," emphasizes the protagonist's unwavering dedication. It suggests that while other opportunities or distractions may arise, their devotion remains steadfast. This theme expands the understanding of sacrifice as the protagonist constantly forgoes other potential romances or attractions in favor of the deep connection they share with their partner.The chorus lyric, "Though I'm a geyser, feel it bubbling from below, hear it call, hear it call, hear it call to me, constantly," introduces a captivating and unexpected metaphor. Comparing oneself to a geyser, the protagonist alludes to a volatile internal struggle. The bubbling energy within represents the intensity of their emotions, which constantly pulls them back to their love interest like a force of nature. This creative use of imagery adds depth to the idea of desire and the overpowering nature of the emotions experienced in this relationship.Furthermore, the chorus continues with the lyric, "And hear the harmony only when it's harming me, it's not real, it's not real, it's not real enough." This line uncovers a profound revelation about the complexities of love and the fine line between pleasure and pain. It suggests that the harmony experienced in the relationship arises only when it causes harm or discomfort. This unexpected theme alludes to the idea that the intensity of emotions and sacrifice within a relationship can sometimes be mistaken for true love. It invites us to question whether the connection we believe to be real may, in fact, be a distorted illusion.In the captivating outro, "But I will be the one you need, the way I can't be without you, I will be the one you need, I just can't be without you," the protagonist reveals their vulnerability and dependence on their partner. This lyric delves into the profound longing felt for their beloved and their own insecurities about fulfilling their partner's needs. It explores the complex dynamic between two individuals in a relationship, where each person yearns to be indispensable to the other and fears their own inadequacy.In Mitski's "Geyser," we are drawn deep into the intricate layers of love and desire. Through unexpected themes and vivid imagery, the song explores the sacrifices made in the pursuit of an all-consuming love. It uncovers the line between pleasure and pain, the inner turmoil that love can ignite, and the constant need for reassurance and dependence. While listening to this hauntingly beautiful track, we are reminded of the complexities of love and the sacrifices we make for our deepest desires, ultimately asking us to reflect on our own experiences of longing and devotion.
