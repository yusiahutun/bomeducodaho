The Best Sol de Janeiro Scent in 2024

September 2024 · 5 minute read

Best for Mood-Boosting

Sol de Janeiro Cheirosa 59 Delícia Drench Perfume Mist

Best for Mood-Boosting

Sol de Janeiro Cheirosa 59 Delícia Drench Perfume Mist

Credit: Sol de Janeiro
Pros
  • Great for gourmand fragrance lovers

Just like a number of Sol de Janeiro scents, their Cheirosa 59 Delícia Drench Perfume Mist is also available as a body butter, but its blend is far less tropical than its predecessor. It uses salted caramel, pistachio, and vanilla notes for an instantly uplifting effect.

“This was about pairing the right gourmand and exquisite woody notes to help deliver a mood-boosting effect,” he previously shared. “The blond woods bring a soothing comfort, while the delicious, caramelized gourmand notes bring an inherent feel-good experience.”

One satisfied customer writes: "The scent is nothing short of amazing! I love the way it transforms me from ordinary to extraordinary!" —Carol K.

Best All-Around Scent

Sol de Janeiro Sol Cheirosa 62 Eau de Parfum

Best All-Around Scent

Sol de Janeiro Sol Cheirosa 62 Eau de Parfum

Credit: Sol de Janeiro
Pros
  • Long-lasting

If Sol Cheirosa 62 smells familiar, it's probably because the eau de parfum features the same fragrance profile as Sol de Janeiro's juggernaut Brazilian Bum Bum Cream. Comprised of salted caramel and vanilla bean, along with soft florals like jasmine petals, this scent aims to celebrate the iconic Girl from Ipanema and Rio during the summer of 1962.

One satisfied customer writes: "Wonderful scent in long lasting perfume form. The smaller version is perfectly sized to carry in your purse!" —Camille D.

Best Floral Scent

Sol de Janeiro Brazilian Crush Cheirosa 68 Beija Flor Perfume Mist

Best Floral Scent

Sol de Janeiro Brazilian Crush Cheirosa 68 Beija Flor Perfume Mist

Credit: Sol de Janeiro
Pros
  • Works for hair and body
Cons
  • Not ideal for those who prefer woody or earthy fragrances

This Brazilian jasmine and pink dragonfruit mist is inspired by the country's 1968 Tropicália movement, which impacted a newfound vibrancy in its music and overall culture. And just like the lush gardens of Rio, Cheirosa 68 is also fresh and floral.

One satisfied customer writes: "I love this perfume so much. Whenever I go out I get compliments and I’ve gone through a few bottles already!! Thank you Sol de Janeiro!" —Jori

Best Gourmand Scent

Sol de Janeiro Brazilian Crush Cheirosa 71 Perfume Mist

Best Gourmand Scent

Sol de Janeiro Brazilian Crush Cheirosa 71 Perfume Mist

Credit: Sol de Janeiro
Pros
  • Works for hair and body
  • Layers well with other fragrances

Be prepared to answer the question, "What perfume are you wearing?" every time you spritz on Sol de Janeiro's Cheirosa 71. Its combination of caramelized vanilla, toasted macadamia nut, and coconut blossom smells good enough to eat, and is a perfect scent match for the gourmand fragrance lover.

One satisfied customer writes: "This scent smells so delicious like cookies! I received a small bottle, but may need a bigger one. It layers so well with other sweet, bakery, or vanilla scents!" —Cyn

Best Fruit-Forward Scent

Sol de Janeiro Brazilian Crush Cheirosa 40 Bom Dia Bright Perfume Mist

Best Fruit-Forward Scent

Sol de Janeiro Brazilian Crush Cheirosa 40 Bom Dia Bright Perfume Mist

Credit: Sol de Janeiro
Pros
  • Works for hair and body

Brazilian Crush Cheirosa 40 Bom Dia Bright is one of Sol de Janeiro's most robust—and perhaps sexiest—fragrances, combining notes of black amber plum, Brazilian orchid, and warm musk. It's scent profile is based on the excitement of Rio's first-ever Carnaval Ball in 1840.

One satisfied customer writes: "It smells heavenly. it has the perfect mix between floral and warm vanilla and is perfect for all year around. I love it so much and was the best decision I’ve ever made!!!!!!!" —Emily

Best Solar Scent

Sol de Janeiro Rio Radiance Perfume Mist

Best Solar Scent

Sol de Janeiro Rio Radiance Perfume Mist

Credit: Sol de Janeiro
Pros
  • Works for hair and body
Cons
  • Not ideal for those who don't like coconut

Sol de Janeiro makes it a breeze to keep a little of summer with you year-round, thanks to its beach-inspired Rio Radiance Perfume Mist. The solar-floral fragrance has top notes of leite de coco (coconut milk), and literal warm sand, with middle and base notes like ylang  ylang  and amber essence that help round it out.

One satisfied customer writes: "Took this perfume with me on vacation to Cancun this last summer and it was perfect for the beach. It has a strong scent of coconut and it just fit the vibe of beachy sand vibes! Absolutely loved it!" —Mackenna
