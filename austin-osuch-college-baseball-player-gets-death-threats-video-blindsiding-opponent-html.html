College baseball player gets death threats after video of him blindsiding an opponent sweeps the web

It was the cheap shot heard 'round the world.

A video that sent shockwaves over the internet, after catching Yavapai outfielder Austin O'Such blindsiding Jake Bamrick, a Scottsdale infielder, standing on second base.

Now, the community college baseball player caught laying out his opponent is receiving death threats since the clip clocked over 1.9million hits on YouTube, prompting him to transfer schools.

Trouble brewing: Scottsdale infielder Jake Bamrick is seen standing on second base as a fight breaks out among the players on the field

Oblivious: As the fight breaks out on the field, Bamrick doesn't see O'Such charging behind him

Yavapai College Athletic Director Scott Farnsworth told ABC affiliate KNXV people from across the U.S. have left voicemails and email messages condemning O'Such's unsportsmanlike behaviour.

And while he doesn't condone his former player's actions (O'Such has since been suspended from the college), Farnsworth said even he was shocked it incited such a visceral response.

'We've reached over 100 emails,' he said. '99% have been derogatory and vulgar. It's been shocking the emotional outburst.'

The video in question was shot during an Arizona college baseball game between the Scottsdale Fighting Artichokes and the Yavapai College Roughriders on March 29.

Knockdown: The force of the collision knocked off Bamrick's batting helmet and left him lying in the infield dirt

Impact: O'Such collides with Bamrick, knocking him to the ground as he races to join the fight

Uploaded to Youtube on April 7, it begins innocently enough, with a sacrifice bunt from a Scottsdale player that drives in a run. But off screen, some sort of conflict develops between the teams, and a bench-clearing brawl ensues.

Axed: Austin O'Such has been suspended from both the league and Yavapai College over the hit against Scottsdale

As the umpires try to break up the fight, Yavapai outfielder O'Such can suddenly be seen charging from left field before laying out Bamrick.

The force of the hit knocked off Bamrick's batting helmet and left him lying flat on the infield dirt â where a team trainer was eventually able to help him back to his feet.

O'Such was suspended for the remainder of the baseball season.

His player bio has since been removed from the Yavapai baseball page.

But the incident sparked furor among users on social networking sites.

Amid the onslaught of anger against O'Such, he shut down his Twitter account, which included such recent tweets as 'I think it's beat down season'.

In addition, a group called 'Ban Austin O'Such from League' has sprung up on Facebook.

Farnsworth told the network he has forward emails threatening O'Such's life to campus police, but the player has decided to transfer to another school at the end of the semester because of the threats.

'Our college and athletic program have been vilified as supporting it or condoning it when we absolutely don't,' Farnsworth said.

He added: 'In talking directly with (O'Such) on why he did it, he admitted it was a lapse of judgment. He's been remorseful ever since.'

Down, but not out: Bamrick was helped back to his feet by a trainer after taking the cheap hit to the back from Austin O'Such

The Athletic Director previously released the following statement through the college's website:

'Yavapai College and the Athletic Department aspire to the ideals of good sportsmanship and fair play. In no way do we condone the behavior as recorded in the video, which occurred on March 29, 2012 at the Yavapai versus Scottsdale Community College baseball game.

'The YC player involved was immediately suspended by the college and will be suspended from play for the rest of the season. The college has implemented this action and will continue to work with the Arizona Community College Athletic Conference (ACCAC) on current and future sanctions following conference procedures. Per conference rules team members from both teams involved in the on field incident have already served their suspensions'.

As for the game, the Artichokes wound up coming back from a 6-0 deficit to capture a 10-8 victory over the RoughRiders.
