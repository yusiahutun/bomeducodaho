Uhuru Gardens: Inside KSh 60b Ultramodern Memorial Park with Hotel, Presidential Library

April 2024 · 2 minute read
  • On Madaraka Day eve, President Uhuru Kenyatta officially launched the refurbished Uhuru Gardens in a glitzy affair featuring fireworks
  • The military, in conjunction with civilians, did a stellar job on the monument, now boasting state-of-the-art facilities
  • The facility, sitting on 68 acres of land, was a detention centre for freedom fighters but is now a national monument

On Tuesday, May 31, President Uhuru Kenyatta officially launched the state-of-the-art Uhuru Gardens after the military and civilians upgraded the facility.

Uhuru Gardens' KSh 60 billion upgrade

Speaking at the launch, the president expressed pride in the project, which cost KSh 60 billion.

"We shall also highlight our culture as social diversity. I am proud to have midwifed the birth of this national endeavour. I am so grateful to God,” he said.

Sitting on 68 acres of land, Uhuru Gardens was once a dark place for Kenya as colonialists incarcerated freedom fighters there before independence.

However, it is now a shining monument of the country’s resilience.

The president ordered the facility’s facelift while giving the military control, and they did not disappoint.

General Robert Kibochi, chief of the Kenya Defence Force, hailed the team of architects, engineers, soldiers and cavils for their outstanding work and dedication.

“They have worked tirelessly to deliver this project.”

Inside the state of the art Uhuru Gardens

The main centrepiece of the monument are buildings in the shape of a shield on Kenya's coat of arms.

The museum's main entrance features statues of freedom fighters Dedan Kimathi and Mekatilili Wa Menza.

Uhuru Gardens also features a tunnel of martyrs in honour of people who gave their lives in the quest for freedom.

Kenya's military history is also on display at the museum, featuring some of their weapons, including planes, mortars, and armoured personnel carriers.

The complex, still under construction, also features presidential libraries, a remembrance hall, the tomb of the unknown warrior and the eternal flame.

Uhuru Gardens will host government offices, a business complex, a convention centre, hotel, among other facilities.

Once fully completed, it will be open for Kenyans to visit and learn about their culture.
