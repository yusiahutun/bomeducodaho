Five International Kenyan Footballers Who Don't Play for Kenya

February 2024 · 5 minute read
  • The Harambee Stars failed to qualify for the Afcon competition, and the team is ranked 110 in the world by FIFA
  • However, there are numerous footballers with Kenyan roots who opted to play for other countries
  • Maybe the national team would be better off with the likes of Divock Origi, Tyler Onyango and Linton Maina playing for them

The concept of nationality seems straightforward. In football, especially, players representing their national team is one of the pinnacles of footballing achievement.

Which Kenyan footballers play for other countries?

In today's increasingly global society, players hold dual citizenship and boast mixed heritage. However, nationality can be as complex as the game itself.

Other than pride and identity, these players opt to play for the country of choice for many reasons, including playtime, international football competitions the country participates in, ranking and lucrative opportunities the footballing nation has, among others.

Still, there are rising cases of players changing international allegiances, choosing the national team of a country where they weren't born, or picking countries they hold ancestral connections to.

This begs the question: what if the players below with Kenyan ancestry chose the spirit of Harambee to be Stars? Could our 110 FIFA ranking be higher?

Divock Origi

Born in 1995 in Ostend, Belgium, to Kenyan parents Mike and Linda Origi, Divock Origi is a Belgian striker on a season-long loan at Premier League club Nottingham Forest.

Origi joined the West Bridgford-based club in 2023, having signed for Serie A club AC Milan on a four-year contract in 2022.

His capping moment was at Liverpool, which he joined in 2015 and won the Premier League, Champions League, FA Cup, FIFA Club World Cup, EFL Cup, and UEFA Super Cup.

When he signed for Liverpool, the then-Football Kenya Federation expressed interest in persuading him to play for the Kenya senior national team. Still, he chose the Belgian side and represented his country of birth at the 2014 FIFA World Cup, ending the Kenyan dream.

Bruce Kamau

Born in Kenya in 1995, Bruce Kamau plays as a winger for Perth Glory in the A-League. He moved to Australia aged four and holds Australian citizenship.

Before moving to Perth Glory, Kamau was on loan to A-League Men club Melbourne Victory from Greek Super League club OFI Crete, which he joined in 2021.

The move to Greece came following his departure from Western Sydney Wanderers at the end of the 2020-2021 season.

He had in the previous season been released by Melbourne City, a club he moved to from Adelaide United, where he made his senior professional debut in the 2014 FFA Cup.

Though he has not been internationally capped yet, Kamau’s trajectory leads him down the paved road to the Socceroos.

Linton Maina

Born in 1999 to a Kenyan father and a German mother, Linton Maina plays as an attacking midfielder for Bundesliga club FC Köln.

The German professional footballer made his Bundesliga debut in March 2018 for Hannover 96 from 2014 to 2022, having played for SV Pfefferwerk Berlin from 2003–2011 and SV Empor Berlin from 2011–2014.

He is currently signed to FC Köln until 2025.

Zech Obiero

Born in 2005 in Redbridge, England, Zech Obiero plays as a centre-attacking midfielder for Leyton Orient. His elder brother Micah Obiero plays as a striker for National League club Wealdstone.

The son of Kenyan former footballer Henry Obiero, Obiero started in Tottenham Hotspur's youth academy and joined Leyton Orient's academy at 14.

The budding star signed a two-and-a-half-year professional contract with Orient in February 2022.

Tyler Onyango

Born in 2003 in Luton, England, to a Kenyan father and English mother, Tyler Onyango plays for Premier League club Everton as a midfielder.

Onyango rejoined Everton after a loan spell at League One club Forest Green Rovers. Before the move to bottom club Rovers, Onyango kitted for EFL League One club Burton Albion on a season-long loan deal in 2022.

Having appeared for the youth international for England at under-17 level, the likelihood of Onyango switching to Kenya is low.

What does FIFA say about playing for other countries?

According to FIFA, any person holding a permanent nationality not dependent on residence in a particular country can play for the representative teams of the association of that country.

FIFA also outlines that players can switch nations provided they have played no more than three appearances for another country before age 21.

However, players with dual nationality may appear in a maximum of three matches for one country and then choose to switch allegiances.
