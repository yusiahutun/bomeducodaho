Woman with Long Beard Laments Difficulty in Finding Masculine Man to Marry Her: &quot;I'm Very Soft&quot;

July 2024 · 3 minute read
  • Harnaam Kaur's facial hair started growing when she was a child, and since then, she has faced a lot of discrimination
  • She disclosed that she wants to get married, but most men are not attracted to her because of her full beard
  • The men who are attracted to Harnaam are very feminine and not her type, as she prefers masculine men who complement her soft nature

Harnaam Kaur, a 31-year-old woman, is in turmoil over her inability to find a husband because of her beard.

How Harnaam's beard affects her love life

In a clip shared on X by @daily loud, Harnaam disclosed that her beard, often deemed unattractive, stands as a barrier to finding a partner.

She said people often told her that she would not be able to get married or have kids because of her mane.

"No one is going to marry you, Harnaam, no one is going to date you..you have a beard. You are not a preference to a lot of men," she said, echoing the words she had heard on countless occasions.

That said, she admitted that she does receive attention from men, except they are often feminine and not her preference.

Harnaam's ideal man

Harnaam yearns for a partner whose masculinity complements her own softer nature, a contrast to her public persona.

"I'll get really feminine men message me, but that's not who I am attracted to. I like really masculine men because I am very soft. I am so different from what you see. At home I am very quiet, I am very feminine...I am very soft. People don't get to see that side of me," she revealed.

Why Harnaam Kaur has a beard

Previously, Harnaam went viral for proudly displaying her full beard.

Kaur, who has faced taunts and criticism for her looks, said she had learned to be comfortable in her skin and accepted herself.
