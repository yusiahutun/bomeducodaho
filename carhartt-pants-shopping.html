Bianca Betancourt, Culture Editor

Faith Brown

“I can’t lie: A pair of Carhartt pants isn’t something I would naturally gravitate to on a normal basis, so when it came time to style them, I tried to think of incorporating elements that felt very me. I ended up borrowing an oversize leopard-print shacket from Kith from my husband’s side of the closet, and paired it with my go-to knee-high boots from Maguire, a black Skims T-shirt, and my favorite gold hoops from Mexican jeweler Georgina Trevino. In the future, I could also see myself trying them with an oversize vintage tee, chunky boots, and a denim trench coat.”

Joel Calfee, Editorial and Social Media Assistant

Faith Brown

“My personal style typically leans more preppy and frilly, as opposed to rustic and functional, but I really enjoyed embracing the workwear aesthetic of these Carhartt pants. They’re comfy and versatile, and I actually tried them a few different ways before settling on my final look. I let them pool around my platform Fila Disruptors, while up top, I wore a thrifted gray turtleneck—which easily tucked into the bottoms. I layered a shaggy brown vest on top that I stole from my mother. Finally, for a playful touch, I grabbed a Maryam Keyhani hat from the Bazaar fashion closet, which ended up perfectly coordinating with the color of the pants. (I just couldn’t help myself from adding a touch of femininity to the look.) I’m excited to try styling these pants again in the future, and seeing how the trending streetwear can add a twist to my other fits.”

Leah Chernikoff, Executive Editor

Faith Brown

“I’ve been spying the cool Pratt kids near me wearing Carhartts for a while, which made me want them and also doubt that I could pull them off. But here is my attempt. I like a looser fit, so I’m wearing them a size bigger than I normally wear, and I like how they sit on my hips and how comfortable they are. Camel and red is one of my favorite color combinations, so I wore them with this vintage cherry-red cardigan (search vintage Scottish cashmere cardigan on Etsy, or J.Crew always has a good one), and to make them feel less casual for a workday, I cuffed them and paired them with these white Le Monde Béryl flats, which have the effect of making every outfit I own look a bit chicer."

Brooke Bobb, Fashion News Director

Faith Brown

“For me, Carhartt pants and jackets always conjured very specific sartorial archetypes. I thought they were reserved for hot, greasy, Supreme-loving skater boys in Dimes Square, or Brooklyn moms who owned fixer-uppers in the Hudson Valley decorated with Noguchi lamps and Eames chairs. It wasn’t until recently that I realized that, despite their mass rise in popularity over the last couple of years, Carhartt pants in particular were pretty versatile; and that, lo and behold, I—someone who is not nearly as cool or unassumingly discerning as those downtown kids or Park Slope moms—could pull them off. I tried on the classic pair and instantly knew I was hooked. I styled them with a vintage Nina Ricci top (though they’d look great with this one from Acne Studios too!) and Dries Van Noten heels.”
