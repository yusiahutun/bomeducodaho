Nottingham Forest 3 - Yeovil 1: Rafik Djebbour's debut goal helps hosts edge past Glovers

May 2024 · 2 minute read

The Algerian striker’s debut goal came right in the in the last minute as he bundled home Simon Cox’s shot.

It completed a disjointed win for Forest but this was their fifth straight league success at the City Ground, extending their unbeaten run to 13 games.

Forest are now within six points of second place and ready for a Premier League push after being backed in the transfer market by chairman Fawaz Al Hawasi.

Djebbour arrived from Olympiakos for more than £1m while Danny Fox,on loan from Southampton, also started.

In addition Jack Hobbs has signed a permanent deal from Hull, Kevin Gomis has arrived on loan from Nice while David Vaughan’s loan from Sunderland has been extended for the rest of the campaign.

And assistant Ned Kelly boss said: “The chairman’s a good fella, we’re all delighted for him and what he has done for the betterment of the club.

“It was a great goal really, off his bum, off his hand and then into the net.

“Djebbour’s a goal poacher, scorer and finisher and that’s exactly what he brings. But he’s trying to get used to everyone’s name. It was three brilliant points.”

Forest were the better side throughout and took the lead 16 minutes in as Henri Lansbury fed Jamie Paterson whose shot was turned in by Byron Webster.

However Yeovil levelled nine minutes later with Kieffer Moore heading in from Jamie McAllister cross.

The Reds were back ahead on 38 minutes when Paterson’s shot from the edge of the area was saved by Marek Stech before Jamie Mackie crossed for Simon Cox to tap home.

Forest should have been out of sight with Mackie missing two golden chances before the break and then Lansbury’s shot was pushed away by Stech after half-time.

Paterson then struck a post from Andy Reid’s pass but Yeovil grew in confidence.

The hosts started to get nervy before Djebbour let them breathe easier again.

Yeovil boss Gary Johnson said: “I thought we had them worried, the crowd had gone quiet, we had a couple of chances and until they got their third we had them on their ropes.

“Little old Yeovil haven’t done too badly given West Ham conceded five and Blackburn and Watford conceded four here.”
