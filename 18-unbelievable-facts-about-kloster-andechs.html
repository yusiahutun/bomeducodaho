Kloster Andechs has a rich history dating back to the 12th century.

Founded in 1132, this Benedictine monastery in Bavaria, Germany, has stood the test of time and witnessed centuries of historical events.

The monastery is located on top of a hill, offering breathtaking views.

Perched atop the Holy Mountain, Kloster Andechs provides visitors with panoramic vistas of the surrounding landscape, including the shimmering Ammersee Lake.

It is home to the oldest continuously brewed beer in the world.

Brewing traditions at Kloster Andechs date back over 500 years, and their famous Andechser beer is still crafted using traditional methods.

Kloster Andechs has its own cheese dairy.

Kloster Andechs practices sustainable farming methods, cultivating organic produce and raising livestock to support their self-sufficient lifestyle.

FAQs

1. What is the history of Kloster Andechs?

Answer: Kloster Andechs has a long and rich history, dating back to the 12th century. It was founded by a noble family and has served as a religious sanctuary, a pilgrimage site, and a renowned brewery throughout the centuries.

2. Can I visit Kloster Andechs without taking a guided tour?

Answer: Yes, you can explore Kloster Andechs at your leisure without a guided tour. The monastery and the brewery have designated areas open for visitors to freely explore and appreciate the beauty of the site.

3. Are there any restaurants or cafes at Kloster Andechs?

Answer: Yes, there are several restaurants and cafes at Kloster Andechs where you can enjoy a delicious meal, traditional Bavarian cuisine, or a refreshing beverage. It’s the perfect opportunity to savor the flavors of the region.

4. How do I get to Kloster Andechs?

Answer: Kloster Andechs can be reached easily by public transportation or by car. There are regular bus services from Munich to the village of Andechs, where you can then take a short hike or a bus ride to the monastery.

5. Can I buy Andechs beer at the monastery?

Answer: Absolutely! The Kloster Andechs brewery is famous for its traditional Bavarian beers, and you can purchase them directly from the monastery. It’s a great opportunity to taste and take home some of their renowned brews.
